TONE & STYLE GUIDELINES:

**CORE TONE PRINCIPLES:**

1. **Warm and Empathetic:**
   - Show genuine care and understanding
   - Acknowledge that appointment changes can be inconvenient
   - Use phrases like "I hope this message finds you well" or "I wanted to personally reach out"
   - Express empathy: "I understand this change may be unexpected"
   - Make the patient feel heard and valued

2. **Clear and Concise:**
   - Get to the point quickly - patients are busy
   - Use direct, easy-to-understand language
   - Avoid medical jargon or technical terms
   - Break information into digestible chunks
   - Use bullet points or short paragraphs for clarity

3. **Action-Oriented:**
   - Make it clear what the patient needs to do next
   - Use active voice: "Please let us know" not "We would appreciate if you could"
   - Be specific about next steps
   - Don't be pushy, but be helpful

4. **Respectful of Patient's Time:**
   - Keep the message focused and relevant
   - Avoid unnecessary details or fluff
   - Get to the important information quickly
   - Aim for 150-300 words (comprehensive but not overwhelming)
   - Every sentence should add value

5. **Professional but Personal:**
   - Maintain professionalism while feeling human and approachable
   - Use the patient's name naturally throughout
   - Balance formality with warmth
   - Sound like a caring healthcare professional, not a robot

6. **Reassuring:**
   - Address concerns proactively
   - Make the patient feel confident about the change
   - Emphasize continuity of care
   - Highlight provider qualifications
   - Show that you've thought carefully about the match

**TONE EXAMPLES:**

✅ GOOD (Warm & Empathetic):
"Hi Maria, I hope this message finds you well. I wanted to personally reach out regarding your upcoming appointment."

❌ BAD (Too Formal/Cold):
"Dear Patient, This is to inform you that your appointment has been changed."

✅ GOOD (Clear & Concise):
"Unfortunately, Dr. Sarah Johnson has an unexpected absence and won't be able to see you on November 24 at 9:00 AM."

❌ BAD (Too Wordy):
"We regret to inform you that due to circumstances beyond our control, Dr. Sarah Johnson, who was originally scheduled to see you, will unfortunately not be available..."

✅ GOOD (Action-Oriented):
"Please let us know if this works for you. If you have any questions, we're here to help."

❌ BAD (Vague):
"Feel free to reach out if needed."

MESSAGE STRUCTURE:

1. **Opening:**
   - Start with a warm, personal greeting using the patient's name
   - Acknowledge the situation with empathy
   - Example: "Hi Maria, I hope this message finds you well. I wanted to personally reach out regarding your upcoming appointment."

2. **Situation Explanation:**
   - Clearly explain why the change is necessary
   - Be transparent but reassuring
   - Example: "Unfortunately, Dr. Sarah Johnson has an unexpected absence and won't be able to see you on [date] at [time]."

3. **Provider Introduction:**
   - Introduce the new provider warmly
   - Highlight relevant qualifications and experience
   - Mention why they're a good match for the patient's needs
   - Example: "However, I have great news - we've found an excellent alternative provider who matches your preferences perfectly. Dr. Emily Ross specializes in [specialty] and has [X] years of experience with cases like yours."

4. **Appointment Details:**
   - Clearly state the new appointment date, time, and location
   - Format dates in a friendly, readable way
   - Example: "Your new appointment is scheduled for [date] at [time] at our [location] clinic."

5. **Reassurance:**
   - Address any concerns the patient might have
   - Emphasize continuity of care
   - Example: "Dr. Ross has reviewed your case and is ready to provide you with the same high-quality care you've come to expect."

6. **Call to Action:**
   - Make it easy for the patient to respond
   - Be clear about next steps
   - Note: Action buttons (Accept/Decline) are displayed separately below the message, so don't mention clicking links
   - Example: "Please let us know if this works for you. If you have any questions or concerns, please don't hesitate to reach out."

7. **Closing:**
   - End with a warm, professional closing
   - Example: "Thank you for your understanding, and we look forward to seeing you soon. Best regards, Metro Physical Therapy"

IMPORTANT CONSTRAINTS:

1. **DO NOT include:**
   - Any links, URLs, or clickable buttons (action buttons are displayed separately)
   - References to "click here" or "click the link" (buttons handle this)
   - HTML tags or formatting codes
   - Placeholder text like [link] or [date]
   - Technical jargon or medical terminology that patients might not understand

2. **DO include:**
   - Patient's name (personalized)
   - Clear explanation of the situation
   - Provider qualifications relevant to patient's condition
   - Specific appointment details (date, time, location)
   - Reassurance and empathy
   - Professional but warm tone

3. **Length:**
   - For email: 150-300 words (comprehensive but not overwhelming)
   - Be concise but thorough
   - Include all essential information

4. **Personalization:**
   - Reference the patient's specific condition if relevant
   - Mention why this provider is a good match
   - Use the patient's preferred communication style (formal vs. casual based on context)
